## Group A: Short Questions (2 Marks)

### 1. Define the term “Sitemap” in the planning phase.
A sitemap is a structured diagram or list that shows all the pages of a website and how they are organized and linked to each other.

- It helps in organizing content logically before the design and development start. 
- It identifies missing or unnecessary pages early.  
- It saves time and cost by reducing changes during later development stages.  

---

### 2. What is the purpose of the “Launch” phase in web development?
The launch phase is the final stage where the website is made live for users.
- It deploys the website on a live server so it becomes accessible to the public.  
- It performs final testing of features, links, and forms . 
- It fixes last-minute bugs.  
- It configures domain and hosting . 
- It ensures security settings and backups.  
- It verifies performance, compatibility, and responsiveness. 

---

### 3. Why is documentation important in the development lifecycle?
Documentation is important because:
- It provides a clear record of requirements and design and functionality of the system. 
- It helps new developers understand the system.  
- It ensures consistency and standardization throughout development.
- It makes maintenance and updates easier.  
- It supports better communication among developers, testers, and clients.
- It reduces errors and confusion during development and maintenance.

### 4. Difference between Front-end and Back-end Development.

**Front-end Development**
- It focuses on user interface and experience.  
- It uses languages like HTML, CSS, JavaScript. 
- It runs in the browser.
- It handles layout, design, buttons, and forms.

**Back-end Development**
- It focuses on server-side logic and databases. 
- It uses languages like Java, Python, PHP, Node.js.  
- It runs on the server.
- It handles data processing, authentication, APIs.

## Group B: Long Questions (4 Marks)

### 1. “Planning is the most critical phase of web development.” Discuss this statement by listing the key activities performed during the planning phase. 
Planning is the most critical phase because it sets the foundation for the entire project.Without proper planning, a website may face issues like delayed delivery, budget overruns, unclear features, or poor user experience. Planning ensures that the development process is organized, efficient, and aligned with the client’s objectives.

**Key Activities:**
- **Identifying project goals and objectives:** This involves understanding what the website is meant to achieve—whether it’s to inform, sell products, provide services, or engage users. Clear goals guide all subsequent development decisions.  
- **Understanding target audience and user needs:** Knowing the audience helps in designing a site that meets user expectations, is easy to navigate, and provides relevant content. This can involve surveys, interviews, or research.  
- **Defining website scope, features, and requirements:** The development team determines what functionalities the website will have, such as contact forms, e-commerce capabilities, multimedia content, or login systems. It also sets boundaries to avoid scope creep.
- **Creating sitemap and navigation structure:** A sitemap outlines all the pages and how they are linked. This helps in organizing content logically, making navigation intuitive for users, and planning for scalability.
- **Selecting technologies and tools:** The team decides on programming languages, frameworks, content management systems (CMS), databases, and other tools required for development, ensuring they fit project goals and team expertise.
- **Preparing timelines and cost estimates:** Project timelines and budgets are drafted to allocate resources effectively. This helps in tracking progress, meeting deadlines, and avoiding unexpected expenses.

### 2. Explain the iterative nature of the maintenance phase. Why is a website never truly finished?
The maintenance phase of web development is the stage after a website is launched, where it is regularly updated, improved, and corrected. 

**Iterative Nature:**
- **Bug fixing:** Errors or technical issues discovered after launch are corrected.
- **Content updates:** Adding new articles, products, or media to keep the website current.
- **Performance optimization:** Improving loading speed, responsiveness, and server performance.
- **Security updates:** Applying patches to prevent vulnerabilities and attacks.
- **Feature enhancements:** Implementing new functionalities based on user feedback or business growth.
  

**Why a Website Is Never Finished:**
- **Changing user needs:** Users’ expectations evolve, requiring updates to content, design, or functionality.
- **Technological advancements:** New browsers, devices, or web standards may necessitate updates.
- **Business changes:** Websites need to reflect new products, services, or branding.
- **Security threats:** Constant vigilance is required to protect against new vulnerabilities.

### 3. Describe the process of “Information Gathering.” Why is understanding the target audience crucial?
Information Gathering in Web Development: Information gathering is the process of collecting all the necessary data, requirements, and insights needed to plan and design a website effectively. This step ensures that the project has a clear direction and meets its intended goals.

**Information Gathering Includes:**
- **Understanding project goals:** Identifying the purpose of the website and what it aims to achieve.
- **Identifying requirements:** Gathering functional and technical requirements, such as features, content types, and design preferences.
- **Researching competitors:** Analyzing similar websites to understand industry standards and best practices.
- **Collecting content:** Gathering text, images, videos, and other media that will be used on the site.
- **Understanding user needs:** Learning about the preferences, behaviors, and expectations of potential visitors.

**Importance of Target Audience:**
- The target audience determines the design, layout, and content strategy. For example, a website for children will have bright colors and interactive elements, while a corporate site will be more formal.
- Knowing the audience ensures the website is user-friendly and meets their needs, increasing engagement and satisfaction.
- It helps in prioritizing features and content that are most relevant to the users, avoiding unnecessary complexity.

## Group C: Scenario-Based Questions (5 Marks)

### 1. You are the project manager for a new e-commerce site. The client wants to skip the “Testing” phase to save money and launch early. Write a persuasive argument explaining the potential risks (security, functionality, compatibility) of skipping this phase.
As the project manager, I strongly advise against skipping the Testing phase. Although it might seem like a way to save money and launch early, doing so introduces serious risks:

- **Security Risks:** Without thorough testing, the website may have vulnerabilities that hackers can exploit, potentially leading to data theft, fraud, or unauthorized access to customer information. 
- **Functionality Issues:** Key features such as shopping carts, payment gateways, and forms may not work correctly, resulting in errors, crashes, or incomplete transactions, which can frustrate users and harm business operations.
- **Compatibility Problems:** The website may not display or function properly on all browsers, devices, or screen sizes, causing a poor user experience and reducing customer satisfaction. 

Skipping testing may save time initially, but long-term consequences such as loss of trust, finanical damage losses outweigh short-term benefits.Proper testing ensures a secure, functional, and reliable website, which is essential for the success of an e-commerce platform.

### 2. A developer begins coding a website immediately after meeting the client, without creating a design prototype or sitemap. Midway through, the client rejects the layout. Analyze what went wrong in the process and how it should have been handled.
**What Went Wrong:**
The developer started coding without proper planning, skipping essential steps like creating a design prototype or a sitemap which caused:
- **Misunderstanding client expectations:** Without a visual prototype, the client couldn’t review or provide feedback on the layout beforehand.
- **Wasted time and resources:** Coding had to be redone when the client rejected the layout.
- **Poor project management:** Skipping the planning and design phases increases the risk of delays and extra costs.

**How It Should Have Been Handled:**
- **Information gathering:** Understand client requirements and target audience first.
- **Planning and design:** Create a sitemap to define website structure and a prototype/mockup for layout and design.
- **Client approval:** Get feedback and approval on the prototype before starting development.
- **Development:** Begin coding only after the design is finalized.

### 3. A university website was launched successfully, but after six months, students stopped visiting it because the exam schedules were outdated. Which phase of the development lifecycle was neglected? Explain the activities involved in this phase.

The website became outdated after six months because content was not updated regularly, showing outdated exam schedules. This indicates that the maintenance phase was neglected.

**Maintenance Activities:**
- **Updating content:** Ensure information like exam schedules, news, and announcements is current.
- **Fixing bugs:** Correct any issues that appear after the site is live.
- **Enhancing features:** Add new functionalities based on user feedback. 
- **Ensuring compatibility:** Update the site to work with new browsers or devices. 
- **Monitoring performance and security:** Keep the website secure and running smoothly.
